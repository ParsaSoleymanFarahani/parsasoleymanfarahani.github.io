About

As a PhD student in Transportation Engineering at the University of Colorado, I focus on improving roadway safety and advancing active transportation systems. My research centers on data-driven approaches to understanding crashes and travel behavior, helping agencies design safer and more efficient networks for all users. I’ve contributed to multiple state-funded projects and published work that bridges technical rigor with real-world policy impact. Alongside my research, I teach undergraduate engineering students in CU Denver’s Computational Methods course, where I emphasize practical data skills and critical thinking. I thrive where data, design, and policy intersect. Whether analyzing complex datasets, mentoring students, or developing new ideas for smarter mobility, I enjoy connecting with others who are passionate about improving how people move and live.
